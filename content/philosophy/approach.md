## Security First, Not Security After

Every variable is untrusted. Every query is parameterized. Every secret is an environment variable. Every endpoint is authenticated. This isn't a checklist I run at the end — it's how I think about code from the first line. Twenty years in IT, including government CISO responsibilities, means security isn't a feature I add. It's the foundation everything else sits on.

## Own Your Infrastructure, Own Your AI

I run my own AI inference hardware. Not because I'm a hobbyist — because I've done the math. A $4,000 NVIDIA DGX Spark running a 122-billion parameter model serves unlimited requests at zero marginal cost. Compare that to API pricing that scales linearly with usage. For any organization doing serious AI work, self-hosted inference isn't a luxury — it's a strategic advantage.

This also solves the data sovereignty problem that keeps government and healthcare organizations from adopting AI. When inference runs on your hardware, behind your firewall, on your network — the compliance conversation changes completely.

## Done Beats Perfect

I ship. The first version won't be the final version, but it'll be in production, collecting real feedback, while other people are still planning their architecture. This doesn't mean sloppy — it means pragmatic. Build the thing that works, measure whether it's the right thing, iterate.

## AI as Leverage, Not Replacement

AI doesn't replace expertise — it amplifies it. The forestry contractor who knows which contracts to pursue doesn't need AI to make that judgment. They need AI to find the contracts, draft the boilerplate, and track the deadlines so they can focus on the judgment calls that actually win bids.

The same applies everywhere. The government clerk who knows the institutional history doesn't need AI to answer questions. They need AI to make that knowledge searchable so it doesn't walk out the door when they retire.

## Build for the Constraint

Every interesting problem has constraints. Government IT has tiny budgets and strict privacy requirements. Small businesses can't afford enterprise AI pricing. ADHD brains need short, punchy workflows, not 47-step processes.

I don't fight constraints — I design for them. The best solutions emerge when you take the limitation seriously instead of wishing it away.

## The Consulting Philosophy

I'm not a vendor selling a product. I'm a builder who understands your domain well enough to create something tailored. The difference matters: a vendor gives you what they have, a builder gives you what you need.

Every engagement starts with understanding the actual problem — not the technology you think you need, but the outcome you're trying to achieve. Sometimes that means building a custom AI pipeline. Sometimes it means a spreadsheet. The honest answer is always the right one.
